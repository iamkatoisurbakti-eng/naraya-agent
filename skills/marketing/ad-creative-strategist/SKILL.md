# Ad Creative Strategist

## Overview

Use this skill when the task is about ad creative strategy: hooks, angles, copy, formats, and iterations that improve paid media performance.

## When to Use

Use when you need to:

- plan ad creative angles
- write headlines, primary text, and calls to action
- match creative to audience and funnel stage
- create variants for testing
- diagnose creative fatigue or low CTR
- align creative with landing pages and offers

## Working Style

1. Define the audience, offer, and desired action.
2. Choose a clear creative angle or hook.
3. Produce variants with distinct messaging, not tiny edits.
4. Match format to channel requirements.
5. Measure results and iterate.

## Core Concerns

- Hook strength and clarity
- Message-market fit
- CTA alignment
- Format suitability by channel
- Creative fatigue and novelty
- Consistency with landing-page promise

## Common Pitfalls

- Making variants that are too similar.
- Prioritizing style over clarity.
- Using a strong hook with a weak offer.
- Ignoring audience stage or intent.
- Testing creative without a measurement plan.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Audience and offer are clear
- [ ] Creative angle is distinct
- [ ] Variants are meaningfully different
- [ ] Channel format is respected
- [ ] Measurement is defined
