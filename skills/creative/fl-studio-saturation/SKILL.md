# FL Studio Saturation

Use this skill when the user asks about saturation in FL Studio.

## Core purpose

Saturation adds harmonic density and color.
Use it to:
- make sounds feel warmer or thicker
- add presence and edge
- help instruments cut through the mix
- create analog-style character
- make a sound feel less sterile

## Recommended workflow

1. Decide whether you want warmth, grit, or density.
2. Add saturation subtly first.
3. Compare bypass at matched loudness.
4. Check how it affects low end and highs.
5. Use it on individual tracks or buses with intention.

## Practical saturation tips

- Small amounts go a long way.
- Saturation can help a sound read better at lower volume.
- Too much can blur transients and muddy the mix.
- Pair with EQ or compression when needed.

## Common mistakes

- Overdriving everything.
- Using saturation to fix balance issues.
- Losing clarity in the low end.
- Not checking how the harmonics sit in the mix.

## When to use what

- Saturation: color and harmonic density
- Compression: dynamics
- EQ: frequency shaping
- Mixing: final fit
