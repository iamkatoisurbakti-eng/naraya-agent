# FL Studio Sound Design

Use this skill when the user asks about making custom sounds in FL Studio: leads, basses, pads, plucks, textures, FX, and synth programming.

## Core purpose

Sound design shapes the character of the track.
Use it to:
- create unique lead sounds
- design bass timbres
- build atmospheric pads and textures
- make risers, impacts, and transitions
- tailor sounds to the genre

## Recommended workflow

1. Decide the role of the sound: lead, bass, pad, FX, or texture.
2. Start from a simple oscillator or sample source.
3. Shape the tone with filter, envelope, and modulation.
4. Add movement with LFO, automation, or subtle pitch variation.
5. Process with EQ, saturation, reverb, delay, or chorus as needed.
6. Test the sound in the context of the beat, not only solo.
7. Refine until it sits in the mix and supports the arrangement.

## Practical sound design ideas

- Leads: bright, focused, and expressive
- Basses: stable low end, controlled harmonics
- Pads: wide, soft, and sustained
- Plucks: short decay, clear attack
- FX: noise, sweeps, impacts, risers, downlifters

## Common controls to think about

- Oscillator waveform
- Filter cutoff and resonance
- Amp envelope (attack, decay, sustain, release)
- Pitch envelope
- LFO depth and rate
- Unison / detune / stereo width
- Saturation / distortion amount

## Tips by goal

- For a bigger lead, add width and harmonic richness.
- For a clean bass, keep the low end tight and avoid unnecessary stereo spread.
- For a warm pad, soften the attack and smooth the high end.
- For movement, automate filter or effect parameters over time.
- For realism or grit, layer a clean sound with a textured one.

## Common mistakes

- Designing a sound in solo and ignoring the mix.
- Too much low-end chaos in bass patches.
- Overusing effects before the core tone works.
- Sounds that are wide but weak in the center.
- Not matching the sound character to the genre.

## When to use what

- Sound design: timbre creation
- Piano Roll: note writing
- Channel Rack: pattern sketching
- Playlist: arrangement
- Mixer: polish and balance
