# FL Studio EQ

Use this skill when the user asks about EQ or frequency shaping in FL Studio.

## Core purpose

EQ shapes tone by boosting or cutting frequencies.
Use it to:
- remove mud
- reduce harshness
- carve space between instruments
- brighten or darken a sound
- make elements fit together

## Recommended workflow

1. Identify the problem area by ear.
2. Cut before boosting when possible.
3. Make small moves.
4. Compare with bypass at matched loudness.
5. Listen in context, not only soloed.
6. Revisit EQ after compression or arrangement changes.

## Practical EQ tips

- Low cuts can clear unnecessary rumble.
- Mid cuts can reduce boxiness or mud.
- High cuts can soften a harsh source.
- Broad curves feel musical; narrow cuts fix problems.
- EQ should solve a purpose, not decorate the chain.

## Common mistakes

- Boosting too much.
- Fixing every issue with EQ only.
- EQing in solo and ignoring the mix.
- Creating a thin sound by over-cutting.

## When to use what

- EQ: tone and frequency balance
- Compression: dynamic balance
- Mixing: overall sonic fit
- Sound design: source tone before mixing

## Good response style

If the user asks for help, return:
- what frequency area to touch
- cut or boost direction
- broad or narrow curve
- next FL Studio step
