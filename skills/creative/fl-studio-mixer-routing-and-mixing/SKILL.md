# FL Studio Mixer Routing & Mixing

Use this skill when the user asks about FL Studio Mixer, routing, gain staging, EQ, compression, sends, buses, or basic mixing workflow.

## Core purpose

The Mixer is where the song gets balanced and polished.
Use it to:
- route channels to mixer inserts
- control gain staging
- shape tone with EQ
- control dynamics with compression
- add sends for reverb/delay
- group related sounds on buses
- prepare the track for mastering/export

## Recommended workflow

1. Route every important channel to its own mixer insert.
2. Set levels before adding effects.
3. Balance kick, bass, and lead elements first.
4. Clean the low end with EQ where needed.
5. Compress only when the sound needs control or impact.
6. Use sends for shared reverb/delay instead of inserting everywhere.
7. Group drums, music, and vocals/effects into buses if helpful.
8. Check the mix at low volume and on different speakers.

## Routing principles

- One sound source per insert keeps mixing clear.
- Label inserts clearly.
- Use buses for shared processing.
- Use sends for ambience effects and parallel processing.
- Keep the master clean; do not overload it early.

## Gain staging basics

- Start with headroom.
- Avoid clipping individual inserts.
- Keep the master from hitting red.
- Balance volume before reaching for plugins.
- If something feels too loud, reduce gain first, not only fader level.

## EQ tips

- Cut problems before boosting.
- Remove mud if the mix feels crowded.
- Leave space for kick and bass in the low end.
- Carve room for the lead melody or vocal.
- Use high-pass filters carefully; do not over-thin sounds.

## Compression tips

- Use compression to tame peaks or add punch.
- Fast attack can soften transients.
- Slower attack can preserve impact.
- Don’t compress everything by default.
- Parallel compression can add density while keeping dynamics.

## Send and bus tips

- Reverb send for shared space.
- Delay send for repeated echoes without clutter.
- Drum bus for group control.
- Music bus for overall tone shaping.
- Vocal/effect bus if the song has voice or lead layers.

## Common mistakes

- Adding plugins before setting levels.
- No clear routing plan.
- Too much reverb on individual tracks.
- Over-compressing the entire mix.
- Ignoring headroom and clipping.
- Mixing too loud and making bad balance decisions.

## Practical mix order

A simple order:
1. Balance volumes
2. EQ cleanup
3. Compression where needed
4. Reverb/delay sends
5. Bus processing
6. Final automation and level check

## When to use what

- Mixer: balance, tone, dynamics, routing
- Piano Roll: notes and MIDI detail
- Channel Rack: pattern creation
- Playlist: arrangement
