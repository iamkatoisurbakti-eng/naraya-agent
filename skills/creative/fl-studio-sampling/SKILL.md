# FL Studio Sampling

Use this skill when the user asks about sampling, sample chopping, audio loops, warping, or turning recorded audio into new musical material in FL Studio.

## Core purpose

Sampling turns audio into new musical ideas.
Use it to:
- chop loops into playable parts
- resample and rearrange audio
- extract texture, rhythm, or melody from source material
- build beats from samples
- layer audio with synths and drums

## Recommended workflow

1. Choose the sample source.
2. Trim the section you want.
3. Detect or match the tempo if needed.
4. Slice the sample into usable pieces.
5. Reorder or rearrange the chops.
6. Tune or pitch-shift if needed.
7. Add drums, bass, or keys around the sample.
8. Process with EQ, filtering, saturation, or reverb as needed.

## Sampling approaches

- Loop sampling: use a repeating phrase or texture
- Chop sampling: slice a loop into smaller pieces
- One-shot sampling: use single hits or vocal fragments
- Resampling: record processed audio and use it again
- Texture sampling: use ambience, noise, or field recordings

## Practical tips

- Clean the sample before chopping if the source is messy.
- Keep the important musical moments.
- Small slices can create new rhythms.
- Pitch changes can turn a familiar loop into a new mood.
- Layer samples with drums or synths so the beat feels original.

## FL Studio workflow tips

- Use the Channel Rack for sample triggers.
- Use Piano Roll when you want precise note placement.
- Use the Playlist to arrange sampled sections.
- Use the Mixer to shape the sample and blend it with other elements.
- If timing is off, stretch or slice before over-processing.

## Common mistakes

- Using a sample without checking the timing.
- Overcrowding the mix with too many sample layers.
- Not trimming silence or unwanted noise.
- Forgetting to make the sample fit the key or groove.
- Relying only on the sample and never adding your own elements.

## When to use what

- Sampling: audio reuse and transformation
- Piano Roll: triggering chops musically
- Channel Rack: quick sample programming
- Playlist: arrangement
- Mixer: tone shaping and glue
