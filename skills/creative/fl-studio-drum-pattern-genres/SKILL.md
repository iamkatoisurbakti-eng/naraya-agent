# FL Studio Drum Pattern Genres

Use this skill when the user asks for genre-specific drum patterns, beat programming, groove ideas, fills, or drum arrangement in FL Studio.

## Core purpose

Drum patterns define the groove and genre identity.
Use them to:
- establish rhythm
- support bass and melody
- create movement and energy
- signal genre style
- build transitions and fills

## Recommended workflow

1. Choose the genre first.
2. Pick a BPM range that matches the genre feel.
3. Build the kick and snare/clap foundation.
4. Add hats for motion.
5. Add percussion for texture and groove.
6. Use fills and variations every few bars.
7. Test the pattern with bass and melody.
8. Adjust swing, velocity, and spacing.

## Common genre starting points

### Hip-hop / trap
- Strong kick and snare/clap grid
- Sparse or syncopated hats
- Hat rolls and triplet bursts for energy
- 808 or bass interplay with kick
- Space is important; don't overcrowd

### House
- Four-on-the-floor kick
- Claps or snares on the backbeat
- Closed hats for drive
- Open hats on off-beats
- Percussion loops for movement

### Techno
- Repetitive kick pulse
- Minimal but strong percussion
- Tension from subtle variation
- Industrial or metallic textures if desired
- Fills should be small and controlled

### Pop
- Clean kick and snare balance
- Supportive hats and percussion
- Simple, memorable groove
- Fill every section without overcomplicating
- Keep it polished and easy to follow

### R&B / soul
- Laid-back pocket
- Softer kick/snare placement
- Humanized hat or percussion timing
- Groove and feel matter more than density
- Space supports vocals and harmony

### Lo-fi
- Soft kick/snare with gentle dynamics
- Slight swing or lazy timing
- Textured hats and subtle percussion
- Imperfect feel is part of the style
- Keep the groove relaxed

### Drum & bass
- Fast, driving break-style rhythm
- Strong snare placement
- Continuous hat motion
- Fills and edits create momentum
- Balance speed with clarity

### Cinematic / hybrid
- Toms, impacts, and big drums
- Sparse but powerful rhythm
- Build tension through rolls and crescendos
- Use percussion to support scene changes
- Avoid clutter; keep impact focused

## Groove tips

- Velocity variation makes patterns feel alive.
- Slight timing changes can create bounce.
- Swing can help the beat feel less rigid.
- Leave room for bass and lead elements.
- Silence is part of the groove.

## Fill ideas

- End-of-bar snare fill
- Kick pickup into the next section
- Hat roll before a drop
- Percussion stop-and-start for tension
- Short tom run or impact hit for transitions

## Common mistakes

- Copy-pasting the same 4 bars without variation.
- Too many drum layers fighting each other.
- No contrast between verse and chorus/drum sections.
- Overusing fills so the beat loses its pocket.
- Ignoring the genre’s typical kick/snare relationship.

## FL Studio workflow tips

- Start in Channel Rack for the core groove.
- Move to Piano Roll for detailed hat rolls and rhythmic edits.
- Use Playlist to vary patterns across sections.
- Route drums to the Mixer early for balance and punch.

## When to use what

- Genre drum pattern: rhythm identity
- Tempo/BPM: speed and feel
- Channel Rack: quick pattern programming
- Piano Roll: detailed rhythm and rolls
- Playlist: section changes and fills
- Mixer: punch, balance, and clarity
