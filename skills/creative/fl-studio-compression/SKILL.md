# FL Studio Compression

Use this skill when the user asks about compression, dynamic control, punch, or glue in FL Studio.

## Core purpose

Compression reduces dynamic range and controls peaks.
Use it to:
- tame loud spikes
- add punch or density
- glue elements together
- control vocals, drums, bass, and buses
- shape the feel of a sound

## Recommended workflow

1. Decide whether the sound needs control, punch, or glue.
2. Set threshold, ratio, attack, and release.
3. Start subtle and listen in context.
4. Compare bypass on/off at matched loudness.
5. Adjust attack/release until the groove feels right.
6. Use parallel compression when you want density without losing transients.

## Compression controls

- Threshold: when compression begins
- Ratio: how strongly it compresses
- Attack: how fast it reacts
- Release: how fast it lets go
- Knee: how soft or hard the transition feels
- Makeup gain: restores level after compression

## Practical uses

- Drums: punch and consistency
- Bass: steady low end
- Vocals/leads: stable presence
- Bus compression: glue for related sounds
- Parallel compression: thicker energy while keeping transients

## Common mistakes

- Compressing just because a plugin is available.
- Too much gain reduction.
- Attack too fast, killing punch.
- Release too slow, flattening groove.
- Not matching loudness before judging.

## When to use what

- Compression: dynamics control
- Gain: input/output level management
- Mixing: balance and tone
- Arrangement: energy shaping
- Sound design: source tone before compression
