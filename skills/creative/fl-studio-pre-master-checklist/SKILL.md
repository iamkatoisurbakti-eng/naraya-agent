# FL Studio Pre-Master Checklist

## Overview

This skill is a final quality-control pass before exporting a mix for mastering or release. The goal is to catch problems that are easier to fix now than after the track is rendered.

Use it as a repeatable checklist, not a creative stage.

## When to Use

- The mix is basically done and ready for export.
- You are sending a track to mastering.
- You want to prevent avoidable problems in the final render.
- You need a consistent final QC routine.

Do not use it for:
- early arrangement work,
- sound design experimentation,
- fixing major mix problems that need normal mixing decisions first.

## Pre-Master Checks

### 1) Session cleanup
- Remove unused patterns, muted experiments, and accidental clips.
- Rename and color-code important inserts.
- Confirm routing is intentional.

### 2) Gain staging and headroom
- Check that the master is not clipping.
- Leave safe headroom for mastering.
- Reduce runaway peaks before export.

### 3) Balance and translation
- Listen at low volume.
- Check kick, bass, vocal, and main melodic elements.
- Make sure the song still works when quiet.

### 4) Dynamics and tone
- Confirm compression is not flattening the mix.
- Make sure brightness is not harsh.
- Keep low end controlled and not bloated.

### 5) Stereo and phase
- Check mono compatibility.
- Watch for exaggerated widening on bass or key elements.
- Make sure the center still carries the song.

### 6) FX and automation
- Verify reverb and delay tails are intentional.
- Confirm automation moves are smooth.
- Check that transitions are not too abrupt.

### 7) Export readiness
- Bounce at the intended sample rate and bit depth.
- Export the correct section.
- Avoid clipping on the final render.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Master already slammed.** Mastering needs headroom.
2. **Fixing everything with the limiter.** That hides mix problems.
3. **Skipping mono check.** Width that sounds cool in stereo may collapse badly.
4. **Leaving a rough automation mistake.** These are easy to miss until the final render.
5. **Exporting the wrong region.** Always confirm start/end points.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Session is clean and organized.
- [ ] Master has safe headroom.
- [ ] Balance works at low and normal volume.
- [ ] Mono compatibility is acceptable.
- [ ] No accidental clipping or harshness.
- [ ] Export settings are correct.
- [ ] Final file matches the intended song section.
